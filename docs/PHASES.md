## Phase 3: Moving in Time (Target: A2.1)
**Goal:** Break free from the present tense — talk about the future and narrate past events.

*   **Chapter 3.1** (ID: `p3_c1`, Length: `medium`)
    *   **Grammar Concepts:**
        * 1. The simple future tense with θα:
        * θα + present-tense stem forms the continuous future (what will be happening).
        * θα + aorist stem (perfective stem) forms the simple future (what will happen, completed action).
        * Example: θα πίνω (I will be drinking — continuous) vs θα πιω (I will drink — simple).
        * 2. Future with common regular verbs: γράφω→θα γράψω, διαβάζω→θα διαβάσω, τρώω→θα φάω, πίνω→θα πιω.
        * 3. Time adverbs for the future: αύριο (tomorrow), σύντομα (soon), μετά (later/after), την επόμενη εβδομάδα (next week).
    *   **Mandatory Vocabulary:** θα (future particle), αύριο (tomorrow), σύντομα (soon), μετά (later), θα πάω (I will go)
*   **Chapter 3.2** (ID: `p3_c2`, Length: `long`)
    *   **Grammar Concepts:**
        * 1. The simple past (Aorist) of regular -ω verbs (Type A):
        * Stress shifts to the third syllable from the end (or the first syllable in short verbs).
        * Endings: -α, -ες, -ε, -αμε, -ατε, -αν.
        * Example: γράφω → έγραψα (I wrote), πίνω → ήπια (I drank), αγοράζω → αγόρασα (I bought).
        * 2. Augment: verbs of two or fewer syllables take the syllabic augment ε- in the aorist:
        * γράφω → έγραψα, πίνω → ήπια.
        * 3. Negation in the past: δεν + aorist form. Example: Δεν αγόρασα τίποτα. (I didn't buy anything.)
        * 4. Time adverbs for the past: χθες (yesterday), πριν (before/ago), την περασμένη εβδομάδα (last week).
    *   **Mandatory Vocabulary:** έγραψα (I wrote), αγόρασα (I bought), χθες (yesterday), πριν (before/ago), δεν (not — negation)
*   **Chapter 3.3** (ID: `p3_c3`, Length: `long`)
    *   **Grammar Concepts:**
        * 1. The simple past (Aorist) of high-frequency irregular verbs:
        * - βλέπω → είδα (I saw): είδα, είδες, είδε, είδαμε, είδατε, είδαν.
        * - λέω → είπα (I said): είπα, είπες, είπε, είπαμε, είπατε, είπαν.
        * - πηγαίνω/πάω → πήγα (I went): πήγα, πήγες, πήγε, πήγαμε, πήγατε, πήγαν.
        * - τρώω → έφαγα (I ate): έφαγα, έφαγες, έφαγε, φάγαμε, φάγατε, έφαγαν.
        * - έρχομαι → ήρθα (I came): ήρθα, ήρθες, ήρθε, ήρθαμε, ήρθατε, ήρθαν.
        * 2. Contrast: using regular aorist alongside irregular aorist in the same narrative to tell a coherent short story in the past.
    *   **Mandatory Vocabulary:** είδα (I saw), είπα (I said), πήγα (I went), έφαγα (I ate), ήρθα (I came)
*   **Chapter 3.4** (ID: `p3_c4`, Length: `long`)
    *   **Grammar Concepts:**

    *   **Mandatory Vocabulary:** πήρα, έδωσα, έβαλα, έβγαλα, έμαθα

## Phase 4: Expressing Nuance (Target: B1)
**Goal:** Describe routines, ongoing past actions, and conditions — move into true intermediate Greek.

*   **Chapter 4.1** (ID: `p4_c1`, Length: `long`)
    *   **Grammar Concepts:**
        * 1. Passive voice verbs ending in -ομαι / -ιέμαι (deponent and passive) in the present tense:
        * These verbs are conjugated with passive endings but may have active meaning (deponent).
        * Type 1 (-ομαι): κάθομαι (I sit), έρχομαι (I come), σκέφτομαι (I think).
        * Type 2 (-ιέμαι): κοιμάμαι (I sleep), θυμάμαι (I remember), φοβάμαι (I am afraid).
        * 2. Using -ομαι verbs for daily routine expressions: Κάθε μέρα κοιμάμαι στις 11. (Every day I sleep at 11.)
        * 3. The preposition με (with) + accusative for accompaniment.
    *   **Mandatory Vocabulary:** κάθομαι (I sit/I am sitting), κοιμάμαι (I sleep), σκέφτομαι (I think), θυμάμαι (I remember), με (with)
*   **Chapter 4.2** (ID: `p4_c2`, Length: `long`)
    *   **Grammar Concepts:**
        * 1. The imperfect (continuous past — Παρατατικός) for ongoing or habitual past actions:
        * Formed from the present stem with past endings: -α, -ες, -ε, -αμε, -ατε, -αν (same as aorist endings but on the present/continuous stem).
        * Key signal words: συνήθως (usually), πάντα (always), κάθε μέρα (every day), τότε (back then).
        * Example: Κάθε καλοκαίρι πήγαινα στη θάλασσα. (Every summer I used to go to the sea.)
        * 2. Contrast between imperfect (ongoing/habitual) and aorist (single completed event):
        * Imperfect: Διάβαζα όταν ήρθες. (I was reading when you came — ongoing background action.)
        * Aorist: Διάβασα το βιβλίο. (I read the book — completed action.)
    *   **Mandatory Vocabulary:** συνήθως (usually), πάντα (always), τότε (back then/at that time), πήγαινα (I used to go / I was going), έκανα (I used to do / I was doing)
*   **Chapter 4.3** (ID: `p4_c3`, Length: `long`)
    *   **Grammar Concepts:**
        * 1. Simple conditional clauses with αν (if):
        * Structure: Αν + present/subjunctive, θα + aorist/present (result clause).
        * Type 1 (open condition — likely): Αν έχεις χρόνο, θα πάμε καφέ. (If you have time, we'll go for coffee.)
        * Type 2 (hypothetical — less likely): Αν είχα λεφτά, θα αγόραζα σπίτι. (If I had money, I would buy a house — introduces imperfect in condition with θα + imperfect in result.)
        * 2. Concession clauses with αν και (even if / although).
        * 3. The conjunction όταν (when) for temporal clauses: Όταν φτάσεις, τηλεφώνησέ μου. (When you arrive, call me.)
    *   **Mandatory Vocabulary:** αν (if), αν και (even if/although), όταν (when), θα αγόραζα (I would buy), φτάνω (I arrive)
*   **Chapter 4.4** (ID: `p4_c4`, Length: `long`)
    *   **Grammar Concepts:**
        * 1. Reflexive pronouns.
        * 2. Using 'ο εαυτός μου' (myself), 'ο εαυτός σου' (yourself), etc.
        * 3. Agreement of the reflexive pronoun with the subject.
    *   **Mandatory Vocabulary:** εαυτός, τον εαυτό μου, τον εαυτό σου, καθρέφτης, κοιτάζω
*   **Chapter 4.5** (ID: `p4_c5`, Length: `long`)
    *   **Grammar Concepts:**
        * 1. The verbs 'to know': ξέρω vs γνωρίζω.
        * 2. Ξέρω for facts, skills, and information.
        * 3. Γνωρίζω for people, places, and deep familiarity.
        * 4. Conjugation and aorist forms (ήξερα vs γνώρισα).
    *   **Mandatory Vocabulary:** ξέρω, γνωρίζω, ήξερα, γνώρισα, πληροφορία

## Phase 5: Nuance and Subtlety (Target: B1.2)
**Goal:** Express possibilities, use the perfect tenses, and enrich your sentences with prepositions and pronouns.

*   **Chapter 5.1** (ID: `p5_c1`, Length: `long`)
    *   **Grammar Concepts:**
        * 1. The Present Perfect (Παρακείμενος): Formed with έχω + the invariable aorist infinitive (e.g., έχω γράψει - I have written, έχεις δει - you have seen).
        * Used for actions completed in the past with results relevant to the present.
        * Example: Έχω φάει, δεν πεινάω. (I have eaten, I am not hungry.)
        * 2. The Past Perfect (Υπερσυντέλικος): Formed with είχα + aorist infinitive (e.g., είχα γράψει - I had written).
        * Used for an action completed before another past action.
        * Example: Όταν ήρθες, είχα ήδη φύγει. (When you arrived, I had already left.)
        * 3. Time expressions for perfect tenses: ήδη (already), ακόμα (yet/still), ποτέ (never - with perfect), μόλις (just).
    *   **Mandatory Vocabulary:** έχω κάνει (I have done), είχα δει (I had seen), ήδη (already), ακόμα (yet/still), μόλις (just)
*   **Chapter 5.2** (ID: `p5_c2`, Length: `long`)
    *   **Grammar Concepts:**
        * 1. Indirect Object Pronouns (Genitive case pronouns):
        * m/f/n singular: μου (to me), σου (to you), του (to him/it), της (to her).
        * plural: μας (to us), σας (to you), τους (to them).
        * Placed before the verb.
        * Example: Του δίνω το βιβλίο. (I give him the book.)
        * 2. Direct + Indirect Object Pronoun combinations:
        * Indirect comes first, then direct.
        * Example: Μου το δίνει. (He gives it to me.)
        * 3. Common verbs taking indirect objects: δίνω (to give), λέω (to say/tell), στέλνω (to send), δείχνω (to show).
    *   **Mandatory Vocabulary:** μου (to me), σου (to you), δίνω (I give), στέλνω (I send), δείχνω (I show)
*   **Chapter 5.3** (ID: `p5_c3`, Length: `long`)
    *   **Grammar Concepts:**
        * 1. The Imperative (Προστακτική): Giving commands and instructions.
        * Continuous Imperative (from present stem) vs Simple Imperative (from aorist stem).
        * Example: Γράφε! (Keep writing!) vs Γράψε! (Write it down!).
        * Negative commands use μη(ν) + subjunctive: Μη γράφεις! (Don't write!).
        * 2. Irregular imperatives: πες (say!), δες (see!), πιες (drink!), έλα (come!), πήγαινε (go!).
        * 3. Using object pronouns with imperatives:
        * Pronouns go *after* positive commands (Δώσε μου! - Give me!) but *between* μην and the verb for negative commands (Μην μου το δίνεις! - Don't give it to me!).
    *   **Mandatory Vocabulary:** πες (say!), έλα (come!), πήγαινε (go!), μην (don't), δώσε (give!)
*   **Chapter 5.4** (ID: `p5_c4`, Length: `long`)
    *   **Grammar Concepts:**
        * 1. Verbs taking the genitive or indirect object pronouns.
        * 2. The verb αρέσει (to like): μου αρέσει, σου αρέσει (it is pleasing to me).
        * 3. The verb μοιάζω (to resemble): του μοιάζει (he resembles him).
    *   **Mandatory Vocabulary:** αρέσει, αρέσουν, μοιάζω, ταιριάζω, φαίνομαι
*   **Chapter 5.5** (ID: `p5_c5`, Length: `long`)
    *   **Grammar Concepts:**
        * 1. Expressing wishes and hopes.
        * 2. Using 'μακάρι να' + present for achievable future wishes.
        * 3. Using 'μακάρι να' + imperfect for unreal/impossible present wishes.
        * 4. Ευχές (standard cultural wishes).
    *   **Mandatory Vocabulary:** μακάρι, εύχομαι, περαστικά, συγχαρητήρια, καλή επιτυχία

## Phase 7: Connecting Ideas (Target: B2.2)
**Goal:** Express possession, make comparisons, and describe exactly how actions happen.

*   **Chapter 7.1** (ID: `p7_c1`, Length: `long`)

        * 1. The Genitive Case (Γενική Πτώση): Used to show possession or relationship (the "of" case).
        * Articles: masculine του (του άνδρα), feminine της (της γυναίκας), neuter του (του παιδιού).
        * Plural for all genders: των (των ανδρών, των γυναικών, των παιδιών).
        * 2. Using genitive to modify nouns: Η πόρτα του σπιτιού (The door of the house).
        * 3. Prepositions demanding the genitive: εξαιτίας (because of), λόγω (due to), εκτός (except).
    *   **Mandatory Vocabulary:** του / της / των (of the), εξαιτίας (because of), λόγω (due to), εκτός (except), ιδιοκτησία (property/possession)
*   **Chapter 7.2** (ID: `p7_c2`, Length: `long`)
    *   **Grammar Concepts:**
        * 1. Comparative degree of adjectives and adverbs:
        * Periphrastic: πιο + adjective (πιο μεγάλος - bigger).
        * Suffix-based: -ότερος/-ύτερος (μεγαλύτερος - bigger).
        * Irregulars: καλύτερος (better), χειρότερος (worse), περισσότερος (more), λιγότερος (less).
        * 2. Superlative degree: adding the definite article before the comparative.
        * Example: ο πιο μεγάλος / ο μεγαλύτερος (the biggest).
        * 3. "Than" in comparisons: using από + accusative or παρά.
        * Example: Είναι ψηλότερος από εμένα. (He is taller than me.)
    *   **Mandatory Vocabulary:** πιο (more), καλύτερος (better), χειρότερος (worse), περισσότερος (more), παρά (than/rather than)
*   **Chapter 7.3** (ID: `p7_c3`, Length: `long`)
    *   **Grammar Concepts:**
        * 1. Deriving adverbs from adjectives:

        * Formal/Scholarly: ending in -ως (e.g., ακριβής -> ακριβώς, ευτυχής -> ευτυχώς, κυρίως).

    *   **Mandatory Vocabulary:** ακριβώς (exactly), ευτυχώς (fortunately), δυστυχώς (unfortunately), κυρίως (mainly), εντελώς (completely)

## Phase 8: Advanced Passive and Participles (Target: C1.1)
**Goal:** Master the passive voice across all tenses and learn how to use passive participles.

*   **Chapter 8.1** (ID: `p8_c1`, Length: `long`)
    *   **Grammar Concepts:**
        * 1. The Passive Imperfect (Παθητικός Παρατατικός): Ongoing past actions in the passive voice.
        * Endings: -όμουν, -όσουν, -όταν, -όμασταν, -όσασταν, -ονταν.
        * Example: Το σπίτι χτιζόταν για δύο χρόνια. (The house was being built for two years.)
        * 2. The Passive Perfect tenses (Παθητικός Παρακείμενος & Υπερσυντέλικος):
        * Present perfect: έχω + passive aorist infinitive (έχω πληρωθεί - I have been paid).
        * Past perfect: είχα + passive aorist infinitive (είχα πληρωθεί - I had been paid).
    *   **Mandatory Vocabulary:** απολυόμουν (I was being fired), έχω πληρωθεί (I have been paid), είχε γραφτεί (it had been written), ετοιμαζόταν (it was being prepared), κουραζόμουν (I was getting tired)
*   **Chapter 8.2** (ID: `p8_c2`, Length: `long`)
    *   **Grammar Concepts:**
        * 1. Passive Perfect Participles (Παθητική Μετοχή): Functioning as adjectives to describe states resulting from an action.
        * Formed mostly with -μένος, -μένη, -μένο.
        * Examples: κουρασμένος (tired), γραμμένος (written), κλεισμένος (closed), ανοιχτός (opened - irregular).
        * 2. Agreement of passive participles with nouns in gender, number, and case.
        * 3. Using participles with είμαι vs. using the passive verb: Είναι κλεισμένο (It is closed/state) vs Κλείνεται (It is being closed/action).
    *   **Mandatory Vocabulary:** κουρασμένος (tired), γραμμένος (written), ανοιχτός (opened/open), κλειστός (closed), αγαπημένος (beloved/favourite)
*   **Chapter 8.3** (ID: `p8_c3`, Length: `long`)
    *   **Grammar Concepts:**
        * 1. Direct and Indirect Speech (Εύθυς και Πλάγιος λόγος):
        * Reporting what someone else said using ότι/πως (that).
        * Example Direct: "Είμαι άρρωστος." (I am sick.)
        * Example Indirect: Είπε ότι είναι άρρωστος. (He said that he is sick.)
        * 2. Reported commands using να:
        * Direct: "Φύγε!" (Leave!)
        * Indirect: Μου είπε να φύγω. (He told me to leave.)
        * 3. Reported questions: using αν (if) for yes/no questions, or retaining the interrogative word.
        * Example: Με ρώτησε αν θέλω καφέ. (He asked me if I want coffee.)
    *   **Mandatory Vocabulary:** ότι / πως (that - conjunction), ρώτησα (I asked), απάντησα (I answered), αν (if/whether), εξήγησα (I explained)
*   **Chapter 8.4** (ID: `p8_c4`, Length: `long`)
    *   **Grammar Concepts:**
        * 1. Passive voice of Type B verbs (ending in -ώ).
        * 2. Present tense: -ιέμαι, -ιέσαι, -ιέται (e.g. αγαπιέμαι - I am loved, γεννιέμαι - I am born).
        * 3. Aorist tense of Type B passive verbs (γεννήθηκα, αγαπήθηκα).
    *   **Mandatory Vocabulary:** αγαπιέμαι, γεννιέμαι, γεννήθηκα, εξηγούμαι, κρατιέμαι
*   **Chapter 8.5** (ID: `p8_c5`, Length: `long`)

## Phase 9: Fluency and Complexity (Target: C1.2)
**Goal:** Express hypothetical pasts, connect complex arguments, and use idiomatic Greek.

*   **Chapter 9.1** (ID: `p9_c1`, Length: `long`)
    *   **Grammar Concepts:**
        * 1. Type 3 Conditionals (Unreal/Impossible past):
        * Expressing situations that did not happen in the past and their hypothetical results.
        * Structure: Αν + είχα + aorist infinitive (Past Perfect), θα + είχα + aorist infinitive.
        * Example: Αν είχα διαβάσει, θα είχα περάσει τις εξετάσεις. (If I had studied, I would have passed the exams.)
        * 2. Mixing conditional types (e.g., If I had done X in the past, I would be Y now).
        * Structure: Αν + Past Perfect, θα + Imperfect.
    *   **Mandatory Vocabulary:** θα είχα (I would have), αν είχα (if I had), εξετάσεις (exams), αποτέλεσμα (result), ευκαιρία (opportunity)

    *   **Mandatory Vocabulary:** ωστόσο (however), μολονότι (although), επομένως (therefore), συνεπώς (consequently), προκειμένου να (in order to)
